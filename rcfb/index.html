About Us

Our Mission

River City Food Bank's mission is to alleviate hunger in Sacramento County by providing consistent access to healthy, meaningful food for all.

RCFB helps people avoid a crisis and work toward self-reliance by providing a short-term food supply when they are unable to meet basic living expenses. Our programs serve low-income families, children, seniors, individuals who are medically fragile, refugees and asylum-seekers, individuals experiencing homelessness, the working poor and individuals with disabilities.

RCFB serves all people experiencing food insecurity regardless of race, ethnicity, gender, sexual orientation, age and background in Sacramento County — with no zip code restrictions.

Emergency Food Distribution

Located in Midtown and Arden Arcade, RCFB's emergency food distribution provides healthy, nutritious food to individuals and families experiencing hunger with our signature "Choice Model" distribution. This model enables guests to select their own food to ensure they receive food that best meets their needs.

The program allows people to choose healthy food including fresh fruits and vegetables, dairy, meat, and shelf-stable commodities. The choice model is a tangible demonstration of our core values — dignity, compassion, respect, health and nutrition.

RCFB serves approximately 5,000 individuals each week.

Eligibility

Anyone who lives in Sacramento County and is experiencing hunger is welcome. RCFB utilizes a low-barrier intake process which does not require ID or proof of income to ensure that all people in need are served.

CalFresh Outreach

More than 74% of CalFresh participants are families with children, showing the immense need for food assistance programs. At RCFB, the CalFresh Outreach team provides the following services to individuals and families facing hunger:

  • Completing applications for CalFresh benefits
  • Rescheduling CalFresh appointments with the county
  • Discovering the nearest Farmers' Market and stores that accept EBT
  • Requesting a replacement EBT card
  • Troubleshooting CalFresh cases
  • Maintaining benefits to meet annual deadlines for renewal

RCFB's CalFresh Outreach staff walk each guest through the process with patience and compassion — explaining each step and answering questions as they arise. Guests can pick up healthy food from our emergency food distribution sites while they visit with the CalFresh team.

Appointments with CalFresh Outreach staff are currently outdoors.

Volunteer With Us

With two locations and a variety of shifts available, we have plenty to offer individuals, groups and organizations looking to give back. Volunteering at RCFB provides the opportunity to work together toward a common goal and have an immediate, positive impact on someone's day.

Individuals

Both Arden and Midtown sites host individual volunteers. Sign up for any shift that works for your schedule — one time or regularly.

Groups

Groups of 3 or more from a workplace, organization, or church can volunteer together — up to 8 people per site per shift.

Families

Families are welcome! Use the individual application to create an account for each family member. Youth 15+ may volunteer independently; ages 10–14 must be accompanied by an adult.

Students

Students can complete volunteer service hours here. Sign up as an individual on CERVIS — the Volunteer Coordinator will sign off on hours as completed. Must be 15+ to volunteer independently.
