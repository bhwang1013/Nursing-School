What is a Food Bank?

🏪

A Food Bank is like a special store…

…where families can come to pick up healthy food for free when they really need it!

❓

Why do families need it?

Sometimes people don't have enough money to buy food. That doesn't mean anything is wrong with them — it can happen to anyone!

🤗

Who can come here?

Anyone who is hungry in Sacramento County. We never ask for ID, proof of income, or your zip code. You're always welcome!

🌟 Our motto is: "Because No One Should Be Hungry"

⚙️

How Does It Work?

1
🚶

Walk In

Families come to one of our two locations in Sacramento. No appointment needed!

→
2
🛒

Choose Their Food

Guests get to pick the food they want — just like shopping! We call this the Choice Model.

→
3
🥗

Take It Home

They take home fresh fruits, vegetables, dairy, meat, and more to feed their family.

→
4
😊

Come Back!

Families can return whenever they need help. We're open 4 days a week!

🥬

Cool fact: Half of ALL the food we give out is fresh fruits and vegetables — because healthy food matters!

Who Do We Help?

Hunger can happen to anyone. We help all kinds of neighbors in Sacramento:

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦

Families

Parents and kids who need a helping hand getting food on the table

👴👵

Seniors

Older adults living on fixed budgets who need nutritious meals

👷

Working Adults

People who work hard but still struggle to make ends meet

🌍

Everyone

Refugees, individuals with disabilities, and anyone going through a tough time

❤️

We help everyone — no matter their race, age, where they live, or what they look like. Everyone deserves food!

How YOU Can Help!

You don't have to be a grown-up to make a difference. Here are things kids can do:

📢

Spread the Word

Tell your friends and family about River City Food Bank. Sometimes people don't know where to get help!

Easy to do!
🥫

Host a Food Drive

Ask your teacher if your class can collect canned food. Even a small box makes a big difference!

Do it at school!
🤝

Volunteer (with a grown-up!)

Kids ages 10 and up can volunteer WITH a parent or adult. You help sort and give out food to neighbors!

Ages 10+

If you know someone going through a hard time, be their friend. Kindness is one of the best things you can give!
